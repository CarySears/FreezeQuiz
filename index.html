What does "thaw" mean?

Thawing means teaching your nervous system that it is safe to move again. It is the shift from stuck, numb, or braced to present, responsive, and grounded.

Safety first

We start with what feels safe, not what feels forced.

Small, gentle steps

Short, doable actions that rebuild trust in your body.

Predictable rhythm

Consistency melts freeze faster than intensity.

Why you freeze (and how it shows up)

When life overwhelms your system, freeze is a smart protective response. It is not a flaw. Staying frozen too long creates patterns like:

Overthinking loops

Chronic analysis that never quite lands.

Emotional numbness

Feeling far away from your emotions and needs.

Always on edge

Bracing for the next disruption or demand.

Hard to decide

Even small choices feel heavy.

Burnout or shutdown

Cycles of sensitivity, collapse, and withdrawal.

Something feels off

A lingering sense that you are not fully yourself.
